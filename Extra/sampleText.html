Chapter 4 - Organic Molecules

Carbon
Tetravalence - 4 bonds can be made Can make different structures Chain - Linear Chain - Branched Ring Also it can form double or triple bonds Carbon can also form isomers Isomers - Same chemical formula but a different structure Example: C6H12O6 forms both glucose & fructose Hydrocarbon skeletons - organic molecules made of Carbon-Hydrogen Chains



Functional Groups
Clusters of atoms that give a specific property to the molecule The Hydroxyl Group <- OH Acts as an Alcohol (polar/hydrophilic) Example: Ethanol Amino Group <- NH2 Acts as a base/ polar Example: Glycine Carboxyl Group <- CO-OH Acts as an acid Example: Acetic acid Phosphate Group <- OPO32- Acts like an anion Often transfer energy Example: Glycerol Phosphate Organic Molecules are polymers (macromolecules) = chains of smaller building blocks Monomers (nutrients) = the small building blocks Many different combinations of polymers using the same monomers

Dehydration Synthesis
A reaction when two and more monomers combine to a polymer. Both monomers were stable. To make them unstable an enzyme removes an OH from one and an H from the other. Then the monomers will covalently bond. H2O is made.

Hydrolysis
Removal of a polymer - breaking down a polymer by adding a molecule of H2O Breaks a bond between two monomers and adding OH, and H to the unstable monomers
